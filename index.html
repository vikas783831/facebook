Frequently Asked Questions

How do I permanently delete my Facebook account? +

Go to Settings & Privacy → Settings → Your Facebook Information → Deactivation and Deletion. Select "Permanently Delete Account" and follow the instructions. Note that it may take up to 30 days for complete deletion.

Why can't I see some of my friends' posts? +

This is likely due to Facebook's algorithm prioritizing certain content. You can adjust this by going to the friend's profile, clicking "Following", and selecting "See First" to prioritize their posts.

How do I stop Facebook notifications? +

Go to Settings & Privacy → Settings → Notifications. Here you can customize which notifications you receive and how you receive them (push, email, etc.).

Is there a way to see who viewed my profile? +

No, Facebook doesn't allow users to see who viewed their profile. Any third-party apps claiming to show this information are scams.

How do I change my name on Facebook? +

Go to Settings & Privacy → Settings → Personal Information → Name. Enter your new name and click "Review Change". Note that you can only change your name every 60 days.

What should I do if I see fake accounts? +

Go to the fake profile, click the three dots (...) on the cover photo, and select "Find support or report profile". Follow the prompts to report the account to Facebook.

How do I download all my Facebook data? +

Go to Settings & Privacy → Settings → Your Facebook Information → Download Your Information. Select the data you want and the format, then click "Create File".

Why was my post removed by Facebook? +

Facebook removes content that violates its Community Standards. You'll receive a notification explaining why it was removed. If you believe it was a mistake, you can appeal the decision.

How do I create a Facebook fundraiser? +

Go to facebook.com/fundraisers and click "Raise Money". Choose between nonprofit and personal causes, then follow the setup instructions. You'll need to provide details about your fundraiser.

What's the difference between a Page and a Profile? +

Profiles are for individual people, while Pages are for businesses, organizations, and public figures. Pages have analytics and advertising features that profiles don't have.
